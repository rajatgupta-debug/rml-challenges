Concept Note

Pack It Right

Three boxes. Limited coins. Things keep changing. What's the smartest way to pack?
Mode: Choices  ·  Band 1 (Grades K-1)  ·  9 screens  ·  ~6 min
Skill
Smart Choices
Math

Real-world
Packing, Spending
Duration
~6 min
Idea Finalisation Checklist
Skill: Making smart choices. Comparing options on what they cost vs what they give you, instead of just grabbing the first or biggest thing.
Core question: There are many ways to pack. Which one's smartest — and how do you know?
Why this matters
AI can optimise any packing problem. But the instinct to stop and compare before picking — that's yours.
Every day, kids grab the biggest, the first, the most obvious. This activity builds the habit of pausing: "Is there a smarter way?" And then, when things change: "Is my answer still smart?"

Interaction Design — Key Patterns
Coin Jar as Scoreboard
Starts full. Coins drop out per box. More coins remaining = better. Kid watches and counts, doesn't add numbers.
Pack Then Compare
Kid commits to a strategy first. THEN sees alternatives. The gap between their choice and the best option is the lesson.
Change Hits Mid-Round
Changes arrive AFTER kid has committed. New toy, sold-out box, oversized item. Each one breaks the current answer and forces re-evaluation.
Scaffolding Fades
S2-S5: compare zone shows alternatives. S6: two options shown. S8 (transfer): no scaffolding at all. Kid applies independently.
Audio & Feedback
🔊 Coin drop Satisfying "clink" as each coin leaves the jar. More drops = more tension 🔊 Toy snap Soft "click" when toy snaps into a box slot 🔊 Box full Gentle "thud" when all slots in a box fill up. Lid closes animation 🔊 Change alert Playful "uh oh!" sound when a new toy arrives or box sells out 🔊 Rule earned "Ding!" when rule card flips in. Same sound as other activities — consistency 🔊 Won't fit Low buzz + red flash when giraffe is dragged to a too-small box
Transitions Between Screens
Round → Change: Packed boxes seal with a lid-close animation. Then the change arrives — new toy bounces in, or box fades out with a red X. Shelf updates.
Pack → Compare: Kid taps "Done." Their packing stays on screen. Alternative strategy fades in beside it for side-by-side comparison.
Compare → Rule: Comparison shrinks up. Rule card slides in from bottom (0.3s). "Next" appears after card settles.
Packing → Transfer: Scene cross-fades from room to party. Same box types, same coin jar. Familiar mechanics, new context.
Transfer → Big Idea: Gifts shrink to icons. Boxes and coin jar assemble in center for final reveal.

Open / To Refine

  • Packing interaction: Tap-to-snap (simpler) vs drag-and-drop (more physical)? K-1 motor skills favor tap. Recommend tap with drag as optional.
  • Coin jar starting count: 10 coins shown? 15? Should match the worst-case total so jar never feels "already empty." Need to math out the maximum possible spend across all rounds.
  • Compare zone depth: Show 2 alternatives or all possible combos? 2 is cleaner for K-1. All combos is more educational but may overwhelm.
  • Constraint round (S6): Giraffe works visually. But "split into 2 pieces" might confuse K-1 kids literally. Alternative: "fold it" or "remove the legs"?
  • Transfer difficulty: 8 → 11 gifts is a bigger number than earlier rounds. Might be too hard for K. Could reduce to 5 → 7 for K, keep 8 → 11 for Grade 1.
  • Replay value: Same toy counts every time. Could randomize counts per round for repeat plays.
  • Voiceover: "Pack these 3 toys!" read aloud for K-1? Reduces reading dependency. Adds production cost.
