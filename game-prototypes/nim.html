Numerical strategy game · Game theory

Nim ♟️

Three piles of stones. On your turn, take any number from one pile. Whoever takes the last stone wins. You play first — the computer plays optimally.

The math idea

There's a hidden rule that decides who wins. Take the XOR of all pile sizes — if it's zero on your turn, you'll lose against optimal play; if non-zero, you can force a win. This is the foundation of combinatorial game theory — and shows up in surprising places, from puzzle design to algorithm analysis.
