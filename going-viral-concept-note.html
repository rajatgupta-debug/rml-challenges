Concept Note

Going Viral

Two videos, same views right now. One's about to explode. Can you tell which one — just from the data?
Mode: Wonders  ·  Band 2 (Age 10-12)  ·  8 screens  ·  ~6 min
Skill
Pattern Recognition
Math
Growth, Doubling, Prediction
Real-world
Media Literacy
Duration
~6 min
Why this matters
Kids share content daily without understanding how spread works. The ability to look at early data and recognize where it's heading — before the numbers get big — is pattern recognition no AI builds for you.
AI can tell you "this video has 220 views." It can't tell you whether 220 is about to become 10,000 or stay at 250. That judgement comes from reading the shape of the data — a skill this activity builds.

The Big Idea
Two things can look the same right now and have completely different futures. The pattern is hiding in the early data — if you know how to read it.

Open / To Refine

  • Prediction interaction: Slider, number input, or pick from options? Number input feels more "real" but slider might be easier on mobile.
  • Data visualisation: Numbers only, bar chart alongside, or a simple line curve? The bar chart in the wireframes shows the shape well — might be enough.
  • Number messiness: Slightly messy numbers (12, 25, 55...) feel more real than clean doubling (10, 20, 40...). How messy vs. clean?
  • Race animation: Does Screen 5 play out automatically (counters ticking up) or does the kid tap to advance each hour?
  • Screen 4 context: Cooking tutorial is a placeholder. Needs something more relatable for this age group.
  • Screen 8 depth: Is the 4-context reveal enough as a 10-second gut punch, or does it need interaction (match context to consequence)?
