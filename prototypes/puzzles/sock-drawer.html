Step 3 · The worst-case walkthrough

Imagine the unluckiest possible streak. Every sock you pull is a brand-new color:

1st
red — no pair yet
2nd
blue — still no pair
3rd
green — you've now seen all 3 colors
4th
Whatever color it is, you already have one. Guaranteed pair.

The math idea — pigeonhole principle

If you have more items than "slots", some slot must hold at least two. With 3 color-slots, 4 socks forces a pair — no matter how unlucky. The same idea proves: in any group of 13 people, two must share a birthday month.

Step 4 · Try it yourself (optional)

Run the experiment. Most of the time you'll get lucky and find a pair on the 2nd or 3rd sock. But the worst case is always 4 — never more, no matter how many times you try.
