Grid puzzle · Math is in the rules

Mini KenKen 🔢

Fill the 3×3 grid with 1, 2, 3 so each row and column has each number exactly once. Each cage (bordered shape) must combine to its clue.

How to read the clues

3+The cage's numbers add to 3.
2−The larger minus the smaller equals 2.
6×The cage's numbers multiply to 6.
2÷The larger divided by the smaller equals 2.
3Single cell — just 3.

The math idea

KenKen layers two kinds of reasoning at once — Sudoku-style logic (each row/column gets each digit once) plus arithmetic (each cage hits its target). The trick is testing combinations against multiple constraints. The same skill underlies real-world problems like scheduling, packing, and balancing budgets.
