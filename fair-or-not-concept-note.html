Idea Finalisation Checklist
Skill: Verifying claims about size. Someone says "this is equal" or "this is more" — can you check if that's actually true?
Core question: These portions look equal. Are they?
Why this matters
Your eyes make instant judgements about "equal" and "more." They're often wrong.
AI can measure for you. But the instinct to question a claim — "wait, is that actually equal?" — is yours. This activity builds that instinct through food portions kids immediately care about.

The Big Idea
Equal doesn't always look equal. And "more" doesn't always mean more. If you can't check it, don't trust it.

Open / To Refine

  • Check mechanics: Grid overlay, pour animation, square counting — each round needs its own. How much to build?
  • R4 (chocolate) vs R1 (sandwich): Both are "shapes that look different but are same area." Keep both or replace one?
  • Transfer round: 2 claims enough or need 3?
  • Rule recap (Screen 6): Needed or skip straight to transfer?
  • Interaction on check: Does kid tap to trigger the check, or does it auto-play after they commit?
  • Sound: Buzzer for wrong gut, chime for right gut?
